## THE SCRIPT

---

### SCENE 1 — OPENING HOOK
**[0:00 – 0:30 | Talking to camera]**

> "If you are a school principal, proprietor, or administrator in Liberia —
> and you are still using paper registers, spreadsheets, or WhatsApp to
> manage your school — this video is for you.
>
> My name is [Your Name], and today I want to show you SchoolSync —
> the first school management platform built specifically for schools
> in Liberia."

**Director note:** Speak with energy. Look directly into the camera.
Show the SchoolSync homepage on screen as you say "SchoolSync."

---

### SCENE 2 — THE PROBLEM
**[0:30 – 1:00 | Talking to camera]**

> "Every term, Liberian school administrators face the same struggles.
>
> Attendance registers get lost. Fee records do not match. Parents call
> asking about their child's grades and you have to go search through
> a pile of papers. Report cards take days to prepare. And when a staff
> member leaves, they sometimes take the records with them.
>
> SchoolSync solves all of this — in one system, accessible from any
> phone or computer."

**Director note:** Pause briefly after "SchoolSync solves all of this."
Let it land before continuing.

---

### SCENE 3 — DEMO: STUDENT ENROLLMENT
**[1:00 – 1:30 | Screen recording]**

> "When a student applies to your school, they fill out an online
> application form — from any phone or computer. They upload their
> passport photo, enter their details, and submit.
>
> The registrar reviews the application, approves it, and the student
> record is created automatically. No paperwork. No data entry."

**Director note:** Show the public application form, then the registrar
reviewing it in the dashboard and clicking Approve.

---

### SCENE 4 — DEMO: ATTENDANCE TRACKING
**[1:30 – 2:00 | Screen recording]**

> "Teachers mark attendance digitally every day — from their phone or
> computer. Each student is marked present, absent, late, or excused
> in seconds.
>
> If your school uses our NFC student ID cards, students simply tap
> their card at the door and the system records their attendance
> automatically."

**Director note:** Show the attendance marking screen. If you have
an NFC card, show a quick clip of tapping it.

---

### SCENE 5 — DEMO: GRADES & REPORT CARDS
**[2:00 – 2:30 | Screen recording]**

> "Teachers enter grades directly into the system for each subject.
> The platform automatically calculates the GPA, class position,
> and remarks.
>
> At the end of term, report cards are generated and ready to print —
> in the Liberian school format your parents already recognize."

**Director note:** Show the grade entry screen, then open a generated
report card and scroll through it slowly.

---

### SCENE 6 — DEMO: FEE COLLECTION
**[2:30 – 3:00 | Screen recording]**

> "The bursar assigns fees to each student based on their class and
> grade level. Parents pay by mobile money or cash. The system records
> every payment instantly, generates a receipt, and shows you exactly
> who has paid and who still owes —
> no more chasing parents with handwritten ledgers."

**Director note:** Show the fee management screen, a student fee
record, and a payment receipt.

---

### SCENE 7 — DEMO: PARENT & STUDENT PORTAL
**[3:00 – 3:30 | Screen recording]**

> "Every student and every parent gets their own login.
>
> Students can check their grades, attendance record, fee balance,
> class timetable, and even download their report card — directly
> from their phone, anytime, anywhere.
>
> Parents never have to call the school again just to know if their
> child is attending class or if fees are up to date."

**Director note:** Log in as a student and show the student portal
dashboard. Navigate through grades and attendance quickly.

---

### SCENE 8 — DEMO: STAFF & ROLES
**[3:30 – 4:00 | Screen recording]**

>
> The registrar only sees enrollment and student records. The bursar

**Director note:** Show the user management or role assignment screen
briefly.

---

## TIPS FOR THE DAY OF RECORDING

1. Practice the script at least twice before recording
2. You do not need to memorize it word for word — know the key points
   and speak naturally
3. Record each scene separately — easier to edit
4. Do at least 2 takes of every scene
5. For screen recording sections, record those at home separately
   on your laptop before or after the studio session
6. Speak slowly and clearly — your audience may not be very tech-savvy
7. Add captions when uploading to YouTube — YouTube auto-generates
   them but review them before publishing

DLS Official Rules
Read before competing

⚽ League Rules

  1. All matches are played on Dream League Soccer (DLS) using default game settings.
  2. Each player represents one team for the entire season — no switching.
  3. Every matchday has a 24-hour deadline. Results must be reported to the admin before it expires.
  4. If a player misses the deadline without a valid reason, the match may be recorded as a 0–0 draw or awarded to the available player at the admin's discretion.
  5. Once a score is submitted and confirmed by the admin, it is final.
  6. Players must use the exact team name they registered with.

⚠ Strict Match Rules — No Exceptions

  1. One match, one result — no rematches. Each fixture is played once only. If players agree to replay after a draw and two different results are sent to the admin (e.g. one sends a draw, the other sends a win), the match is automatically recorded as a DRAW regardless of any private agreement between the two players. Submitting multiple results for the same fixture is not permitted under any circumstance.
  2. No personal scheduling within the 24-hour window. The match window runs from 12:00am to 12:00am (midnight to midnight). Both players are required to play at any time their opponent is available within that window. You may not set your own preferred time and refuse to play because your opponent is ready at a different hour. As long as the 24-hour deadline has not passed, refusing to play when your opponent is available is a violation — the admin may award the match to the ready player.

⏱ Postponement Rules

  1. A match can only be postponed if both players agree and notify the admin before the deadline.
  2. Postponed matches must be played within 48 hours of the original deadline.
  3. Each player is allowed a maximum of 2 postponements per season.
  4. The admin has the final say on whether a postponement is granted.

🔌 Internet Disconnect Rules

  1. A goal has been scored before disconnect: The leading player wins immediately. No replay under any circumstance. It does not matter what minute the disconnect happened — once a goal is on the board, the result is final.
  2. Score is 0–0, disconnect before 60 min: Full replay. One chance only — both players must be ready with a stable connection before the replay starts.
  3. Score is 0–0, disconnect at 60+ min: The opponent (the player who stayed) wins. The disconnecting player cannot cause a loss for someone who did nothing wrong.
  4. Double disconnect: If a player disconnects twice in the same match, the opponent wins automatically regardless of time or score.
  5. Screenshot requirement: Both players must screenshot the game showing the scoreline, game clock, and both team names. Send to admin on WhatsApp within 10 minutes. A single screenshot is not accepted — both screenshots must match.
  6. Your internet is your responsibility. Fix your connection before the match starts. Disconnecting after conceding a goal to force a replay is considered cheating and results in immediate disqualification.

🏆 Promotion & Relegation

  1. At the end of the season, the top 8 players from Division 1 qualify for the Champions League.
  2. The bottom 3 players from Division 1 are relegated to Division 2.
  3. Promotion and relegation are determined by final league table position (Points → Goal Difference → Goals Scored).
  4. In the event of a tie on all criteria, a playoff match may be arranged at the admin's discretion.

⚖ Fair Play

  1. Any form of cheating, score manipulation, or unsportsmanlike conduct will result in immediate disqualification.
  2. Treat all opponents with respect — harassment of any kind will not be tolerated.
  3. The admin's decision is final in all disputes.
